# Chapter 8: Service Consumption and Portfolio

## Core Idea
Service Consumption describes what consumers receive; Manage Portfolio groups and governs service-related investments and offerings.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Service / Offering Pattern**: Model a broad service, then offerings that express specific commitments, channels, or consumer choices.
- **Portfolio Governance**: Group related services and investments for ownership and decision-making.

## Key Concepts
- **Business Service**: Service delivering value to business consumers.
- **Business Service Offering**: Specific consumable variation of a business service.
- **Request Catalog**: Consumer request channel, not the service model itself.
- **Portfolio**: Governed collection of related items.
- **Service Portfolio**: Portfolio focused on services.

## Mental Models
- Use service for enduring value proposition; use offering for differentiated commitment.
- Keep catalog items as request mechanisms linked to offerings.
- Use portfolios for governance, not operational dependency mapping.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating catalog items as services**: Confuses request experience with delivered value.
- **Creating offerings without meaningful differentiation**: Adds maintenance without decision value.
- **Using portfolio hierarchy as runtime topology**: Portfolios and dependency maps solve different problems.

## Worked Example
Model "Employee Collaboration" as a business service. Create standard and premium offerings with different support commitments. Link catalog requests to offerings, operational services to delivery, and the service to a service portfolio.

## Key Takeaways
1. Separate service, offering, and catalog item.
2. Model consumption around value and commitments.
3. Use portfolios to govern related services.
