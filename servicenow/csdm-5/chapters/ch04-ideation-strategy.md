# Chapter 4: Ideation and Strategy

## Core Idea
The Ideation & Strategy domain represents why investment or change should happen before detailed design and delivery begin.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Strategy-to-Execution Traceability**: Connect ideas, priorities, goals, targets, and planning items to downstream design and delivery.

## Key Concepts
- **Product Idea**: Proposed opportunity or improvement.
- **Planning Item**: Work considered or selected for planning.
- **Strategic Priority**: Area receiving organizational focus.
- **Goal**: Desired outcome.
- **Target**: Measurable result supporting a goal.

## Mental Models
- Use goals and targets to explain why downstream objects exist.
- Keep strategic intent separate from solution design.
- Trace approved ideas into planning and design records.

## Anti-patterns
- **Starting at implementation**: Loses rationale and outcome traceability.
- **Treating every request as strategy**: Strategy records should capture meaningful intent and priorities.

## Worked Example
A goal to reduce customer onboarding time produces a measurable target. A product idea proposes automation. Once approved, a planning item connects that intent to a business application and subsequent delivery work.

## Key Takeaways
1. Capture intent before design.
2. Use measurable targets.
3. Connect approved strategy to downstream records.
